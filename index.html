The Life Story of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

Birth Childhood Prophethood Miracles Death

Birth of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

Hazrat Musa (A.S.) was born during a time when the Pharaoh of Egypt was oppressing the children of Israel. It was prophesied that a child from the children of Israel would rise and overthrow Pharaoh. To protect Musa, his mother placed him in a basket and set him afloat on the Nile River, where he was later found by Pharaoh's wife, Asiya.

Childhood of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

Musa grew up in Pharaoh's palace, unaware of his true heritage. He was raised by Pharaoh's wife Asiya, who loved him dearly. When he grew older, Hazrat Musa (A.S.) became aware of the oppression of his people, the Israelites, and one day he killed an Egyptian man who was mistreating a Hebrew slave.

The Prophethood of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

After fleeing to Midian to escape the punishment for the killing, Hazrat Musa (A.S.) encountered a burning bush where Allah (SWT) spoke to him and appointed him as a prophet. He was commanded to return to Egypt and demand the release of the Israelites from Pharaoh's tyranny.

Miracles of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

Hazrat Musa (A.S.) performed many miracles by the command of Allah, such as turning his staff into a serpent, parting the Red Sea, and bringing forth water from a rock. These miracles were meant to prove his prophethood and Allah's power, but Pharaoh and his people rejected him.

The Death of Hazrat Musa (A.S.)

Hazrat Musa (A.S.) led the Israelites to freedom, and they wandered the desert for many years before reaching the promised land. He passed away before entering it, but his legacy lives on as one of the greatest prophets in Islam. His story is an inspiration to millions.
